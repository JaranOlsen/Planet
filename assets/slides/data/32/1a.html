Attunement (Empathy's Accuracy)

  • What it is:
    • Moment-to-moment tracking of the client's affect, body, and meaning.
    • Matching pace and intensity while staying differentiated (not merged).
  • Why it matters:
    • Increases empathic accuracy and helps the client feel met.
    • Guides interventions toward what is alive now (not just the narrative).
  • Practice:
    • Track cues: voice, breath, gaze, posture, hesitations, contradictions.
    • Reflect feeling + meaning (tentatively), then check for fit.
    • Notice misattunement early and repair explicitly.
  • Common drift:
    • Mind-reading (certainty) instead of checking (collaboration).
    • Over-mirroring intensity without containment or meaning.
Repair line
"I think I missed you. Can we back up?"
"What's the most important part for me to get?"
