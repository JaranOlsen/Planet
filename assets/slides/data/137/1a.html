Wisdom (Empathy's Timing and Dose)

  • What it is:
    • Clinical judgment about timing, intensity, and direction of response.
    • Knowing when to reflect, when to invite focusing, and when to slow down.
  • Why it matters:
    • Keeps the client within their window of tolerance.
    • Prevents premature interpretation, advice, or pushing for insight.
  • Practice:
    • Choose the next smallest helpful step (then watch what it produces).
    • Do one intervention at a time; adjust based on the client's response.
    • Get consent for depth: "Do you want to stay with this a bit?"
  • Common drift:
    • Over-intervening to manage therapist anxiety.
    • Confusing conceptual clarity with experiential change.
3 quick checks
Is the client resourced enough for this depth?
Am I following the client's process or my agenda?
What do I expect in the next 30 seconds?
