Authenticity (Empathy Without Performance)

  • What it is:
    • Congruence: your inner response and outward stance are aligned.
    • Genuine contact in service of the client (not a professional mask).
  • Why it matters:
    • Builds trust and deepens the therapeutic alliance.
    • Models self-contact and makes repair possible when you miss.
  • Practice:
    • Name process sparingly: "I want to check if I'm with you."
    • Use brief self-disclosure only with clear clinical purpose and clean boundaries.
    • Own mistakes quickly; repairs often strengthen the relationship.
  • Common drift:
    • Oversharing, rescuing, or shifting focus to the therapist.
    • Hiding behind technique to avoid real contact or conflict.
Helpful lines
"I may be off. What fits better?"
"Can we slow down and stay with what's here?"
"I'm noticing uncertainty in me, and I want to check it with you."
