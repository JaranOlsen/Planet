Wisdom

  • Therapeutic wisdom: Applying understanding of the client's experiential world to promote insight and growth.
  • Important for guiding focusing-oriented interventions and responses.
  • Practice by:
    • Combining knowledge of focusing, experiential process, and the client's unique world to facilitate felt shift.
    • Practicing humility, acknowledging the client as the expert of their own experience.
    • Engaging in reflective practices, enriching therapeutic wisdom and enhancing empathy.
"Wisdom, unlike intelligence, demands not just more brainpower, but also more heart and soul." - Erik H. Erikson, The Life Cycle Completed (1982)
