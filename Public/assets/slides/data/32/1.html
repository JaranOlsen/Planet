Attunement

  • Central to empathic understanding and facilitation of emotion-focused work.
  • Promotes emotional resonance, facilitating experiential depth and exploration of felt sense.
  • Practice by:
    • Recognizing and mirroring subtle shifts in the client's emotional and embodied experience.
    • Validating emotions to help the client process and integrate them.
    • Using therapist's embodied attunement as a therapeutic tool, contributing to the focusing process.
"Attunement is a primary aspect of therapeutic work, based in the human capacity for empathy and regulated by one's own emotional state and by one's sense of the other's emotional state" - Diana Fosha, The Transforming Power of Affect (2000)
