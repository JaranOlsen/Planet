Authenticity

  • Congruence: a key Rogerian condition promoting trust and therapeutic alliance.
  • Contributes to the client's self-congruence and authenticity.
  • Practice by:
    • Practicing transparency and integrity, modeling authenticity.
    • Using appropriate self-disclosure to enhance the therapeutic relationship and facilitate focusing.
    • Recognizing personal reactions, addressing them if therapeutically relevant and helpful.

When the therapist is genuine, it means that the therapist is being what he actually is during the relationship, not wearing a professional mask." - Carl Rogers, On Becoming a Person (1961)
