Presence

  • A mindful, accepting presence is key to the focusing-oriented therapy.
  • Fosters clients' mindfulness and inner-directedness.
  • Practice by:
    • Cultivating personal mindfulness practices to enhance presence and openness to emerging experiences.
    • Demonstrating interest and attentiveness to the unfolding client's inner process.
    • Holding space for silence, facilitating the client's focusing process.
"As I 'sit with' my patient in mindful awareness... I can attune to the subtlest aspects of her narrative, the nuanced shifts in her facial expression, the fleeting hints of trauma-related dissociation in her gaze." - Ronald D. Siegel, The Mindfulness Solution (2010)
