Presence (Empathy's Ground)

  • What it is:
    • Steady, non-hurried attention to the client's moment-to-moment experience.
    • Capacity to stay with emotion, silence, and uncertainty without escaping.
  • Why it matters:
    • Clients borrow your nervous system; steadiness makes depth possible.
    • Creates space for felt sense to emerge before meaning-making.
  • Practice:
    • Ground first (feet, breath, pace) before you intervene.
    • Pause after key moments; let meaning land.
    • Invite embodiment: "As you say that, what do you notice inside?"
  • Common drift:
    • Filling space with questions or advice to avoid silence.
    • Tracking story content while missing affect and micro-shifts.
Presence cues
Slower pace, fewer words, warmer tone.
More tracking of breath, posture, and shifts.
Willingness to wait.
