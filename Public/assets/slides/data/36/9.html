Withdrawal symptoms

  • Depression: A significant drop in mood and motivation, often accompanied by feelings of hopelessness and sadness.
  • Anxiety: Increased feelings of nervousness, tension, or panic.
  • Fatigue: Extreme tiredness and a lack of energy or motivation to engage in daily activities.
  • Sleep disturbances: Insomnia or excessive sleepiness, along with changes in sleep patterns.
  • Cognitive impairments: Difficulties with concentration, memory, and decision-making.
  • Physical symptoms: These can include headaches, dizziness, changes in appetite, and gastrointestinal issues.
  • Mood swings: Rapid and intense changes in mood.
  • Cravings: Intense desire to re-engage with the substance or behavior to relieve withdrawal symptoms.
  • Irritability or agitation: Increased frustration or anger over minor issues.
  • Anhedonia: Inability to feel pleasure, leading to a lack of interest in activities that used to be enjoyable.
