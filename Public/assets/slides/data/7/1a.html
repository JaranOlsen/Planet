Empathy (The Parent Skill)

  • What it is:
    • Accurate, emotionally resonant understanding of the client's inner experience.
    • Communicated and checked: the client confirms, corrects, and deepens.
  • What it does:
    • Lowers threat and shame; strengthens safety and alliance.
    • Supports experiential processing and felt shift (change that is sensed, not just understood).
  • Built from (facets):
    • Presence
    • Attunement
    • Unconditional positive regard
    • Authenticity
    • Wisdom (timing and dose)
  • Practice:
    • Reflect more than you ask; speak tentatively ("Is it like...?").
    • Track markers of fit; update quickly when you're off.
    • Name and repair misattunements; repairs often deepen empathy.
In-session markers of fit
Client corrects you and goes deeper (not defensive).
Breath softens, affect shifts, or the story becomes more embodied.
The client signals recognition: "Yes. That's it."
