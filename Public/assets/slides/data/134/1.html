Unconditional Positive Regard

  • Rogers' core condition, integral for creating a validating therapeutic environment promoting self-acceptance and personal growth.
  • Mitigates conditions of worth, enabling the exploration and transformation of felt sense.
  • Practice by:
    • Conveying genuine respect for the client's subjective experience and inherent worth.
    • Checking biases and judgments to fully embrace the client's phenomenological field.
    • Verbalizing understanding and acceptance, reinforcing the client's intrinsic value and fostering self-acceptance.
"When the therapist is experiencing a positive, acceptant attitude toward whatever the client is at that moment, therapeutic movement or change is more likely." - Carl Rogers, On Becoming a Person (1961)
