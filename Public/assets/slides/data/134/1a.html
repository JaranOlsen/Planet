Unconditional Positive Regard (Empathy Without Conditions)

  • What it is:
    • Non-contingent respect and warmth toward the client as a person.
    • Understanding behavior without shaming the client (understanding is not approval).
  • Why it matters:
    • Counters shame and "conditions of worth", making disclosure safer.
    • Helps disowned parts come into awareness without collapse or defensiveness.
  • Practice:
    • Assume protectiveness: "This makes sense as a way you've coped."
    • Watch micro-judgments; return to curiosity and dignity.
    • Hold compassion with clarity: validate emotion and explore impact and choice.
  • Common drift:
    • Collusion (avoiding accountability) or cold neutrality (withholding warmth).
    • Using "acceptance" to bypass needed limits or difficult truths.
Distinction
UPR values the client.
It does not require agreement with every action.
