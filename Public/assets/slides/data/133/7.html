Early breakthrough

This is the case of a 23-year-old woman who suffered a severe brain injury in a car accident in July 2005. She was left in a non-responsive state, or ‘wakeful unawareness’. She could open her eyes, had cycles of sleep, and wakefulness. However, she did not respond to commands and couldn’t perform voluntary movements. She stayed in this state for the next five months. They then observed the brain activity of the woman and compared it to healthy controls. The pattern was the same.
